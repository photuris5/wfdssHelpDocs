July 2025

WFDSS NextGen 2025 Release Update

July 2025

As we pass the 90-day mark of the implementation of WFDSS NG as the system of record for Federal wildland fire incidents, the WFDSS team has been as busy as ever with a focus on improving user experience and adding functionality.

WHAT'S NEW?

On June 12th, WFDSS v1.0.2 was released, marking the second minor release after the initial v1.0 release. Minor releases generally address identified bugs and application maintenance and updates. The Release Notes can be found on the WFDSS Homepage under What’s New. The team is working to provide new releases every 4-6 weeks going forward to be responsive to the field.

WHAT WE'RE WORKING ON

The development team is working on the automated system validation of required elements of the decision, incorporating the Fire Modeling Services Framework v2 for more agile modeling, updating URLs, displaying FSPro status updates, coverage validation for LANDFIRE data layers, and an upgrade to the ArcServer. The first pieces of the second fire behavior model (FARSITE) are also starting to move through the system.

The features we are excited to release later in 2025 include approving decisions with multiple approvers, incorporating the remaining fire behavior models (FlamMap and MTT), the ability to copy an analysis, User and System Notifications, Incident History, creating an incident in EDU (Non-IRWIN records), ERC climatology graphs, and more!

The services that WFDSS relies on are also undergoing enhancements and we look forward to connecting to FMSF v2, FEMS 3.2 and LFPS upgrades. It takes a lot of communication between the teams to keep the systems and services aligned - we couldn’t do it without them!

March 2025

WFDSS NextGen 2025 Release Update

March 2025

WFDSS NextGen development is in the final stage ahead of becoming the system of record. The next 3 weeks will be exciting as we wrap up feature development for the first release (1.0). The code lock for the 1.0 release has begun and over the next 3 weeks the WFMRD&A will be engaging in robust testing, supporting training and course content development, as well as inviting users to participate in application testing and providing feedback, all in preparation of the full transition to WFDSS NextGen on March 26th.

The current operational version of WFDSS will be maintained in its current state of Operations and Maintenance (O&M) with no Development Modernization or Enhancements (DME) taking place. Only patching and necessary technical upgrades and changes necessary to comply with CIO policy and hosting environment requirements have been occurring as we continue to prepare for WFDSS Classic's archiving.

WFDSS NextGen EDU (Training) is now available for user training. While all the features have not made their way to the training environment they will be soon.

It is important to note that some features that were intended to be in the 1.0 release had to be pushed out in order to make the March 26th deadline. Namely Multiple Approvers and system validation of required elements of the decision have had to be pushed to the 1.1 release which is slated for end of April or Mid-May depending on o development cadence. Other often used features in Classic that won't be included in the 1.0 release that will be released in FY2025 include: FARSITE, FlamMap, MTT, the ability to copy an analysis, User and System Notifications, and Incident History

NOTE: As we approach production launch for 1.0 EDU will need to be tested extensively.  Thank you in advance for your patience as we work out the kinks and if you have the bandwidth, please contact us to assist in testing and stabilization activities.

We look forward to hearing your feedback!

January 2025

WFDSS NextGen development is in the final stage ahead of becoming the system of record. The next 3 months will be exciting as we wrap up feature development for the first release (1.0). Over the next 3 months and WFMRDA will be engaging in robust testing, supporting training and course content development, as well as inviting users to participate in application testing and provide feedback, all in preparation of the full transition to WFDSS NextGen on March 26th.

September 2024

WFDSS NextGen’s First Production Release is Planned for Spring 2025!

September 2024

Over the next 6 months there will be many moving parts that will need to come together to meet the planned release date window. As a result, the exact release date will be flexible based on several interrelated factors including:

  • The provisioning, configuration and access of the production environments: Operational Acceptance Testing (OAT), Training (EDU), and Production (PROD)
  •  User Access to User Acceptance Testing (UAT) and Training (EDU)
  • Minimum Viable Product (MVP) Completion 
  • Interoperability testing, individual system updates and availability of several dependent services and applications including the Fire Environment Mapping Service (FEMS), the LANDFIRE Production Service (LFPS), the Fire Modeling Service Framework (FMSF), the Wildland Fire Application Portal (FAMAuth), and the Spatial Fire Planning Service (SPFS), to name a few. 

Through the fall, winter and into the spring, users will be getting frequent communication keeping them up to date on what is happening in regard to the factors above. In order to stay informed, please keep an eye out for emails from WFDSS announcing system demos, feature feedback sessions, trainings and suggested preparations for the 2025 season.   If you are interested in providing feedback or signing up for a user group, you can find more information on the WFDSS NextGen Home/Help Page.

 

October 2023

NextGen Development, User Testing and Feedback

October 2023

The NextGen team is happy to announce that the effort to update WFDSS has moved another step forward!

The new user interface as well as the underlying system architecture is ready for limited user testing.  At this time, only those users that have GAE roles in WFDSS Classic will be granted roles and invited to get a first look at the new environment. Because these users will be the ones tasked with approving accounts for others, it made sense to get them into the system first so that we could work any bugs out of the account approval process.

WARNING: This is a development environment with changes/updates occurring daily.  Not all functionality is available at this time and many of the issues you may encounter are known and in process.  Thank you in advance for your patience as we work out the kinks. 

We want to hear from you.

  • Submit feedback related to appearance and functionality at WFDSS NextGen Feedback.

  • Before submitting your feedback, please double check your issue hasn't already been captured in the Feedback Inventory, which will be updated periodically.

  • Presentations and user feedback from previous User Group sessions are available at NextGen WFDSS.

When we are able to open the application to all users for testing, we will solicit users across the existing WFDSS Classic user community.

We look forward to hearing your feedback!
