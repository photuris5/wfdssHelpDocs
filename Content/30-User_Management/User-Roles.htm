User Roles

Roles in WFDSS NextGendiffer from those Classic WFDSS. NextGen users hold one role and roles have hierarchical permissions so every role builds upon the previous role. To change your role, contact your GA Editor.

This is how the roles build. The default role assigned to users with initial system access is the Viewer role. If appropriate, your GA Editor can elevate your role to Incident Editor or Fire Behavior Analyst as needed.

Viewer

When you select WFDSS NextGen in iNAP for the first time, you are granted a Viewer role when you have a recognized email address. GA Editors will grant usersfurther role access. This role is analogous to the Anonymous role in WFDSS classic and can do the following:

  • Edit the user's own profile.

  • View the GAE Staff report for the purposes of getting their roles elevated.

Incident Editor

This will be the most common role in WFDSS NextGen. It will combine Classic WFDSS roles of Viewer and Author. Permissions will be flattened in a way that some users will see an increase in system privileges putting a larger onus on users to respect and abide by regional and local protocols, and their neighborsâ€™ protocols while giving users the maximum ability to manage incidents without multiple roles and complicated interactions. Editors can:

  • Browse for Incidents

  • View any published decisions and analyses that are statused as: Retain for reference, Calibration, Complete for Operational Use, or Complete for Strategic Use

  • Initiate a record on any fire anywhere in the nation

  • Add collaborators to an incident for which they are an collaborator, including Analysts and Approvers

  • Change themselves or any other collaborator to the lead collaborator

  • As a lead collaborator send a decision for approval.

  • View draft decision content on any incident they are a collaborator of

Fire Behavior Analyst

The Analyst role in WFDSS NextGen is intended for those who need to be able to run fire behavior analysis (FlamMap, MTT, FARSITE, and FSPro) for decision support. Fire Behavior Analyst will be the next role upfrom Incident Editor in WFDSS NextGen, so because the roles in NextGen are additive this role will build upon the Editor role. In addition to the Incident Editor privileges, Analysts will be able to:

  • Collaborate on any incident without being added (implicit collaborator)

  • Run analyst assisted fire behavior analysis on any incident

  • Status the results of their own fire behavior analysis

  • Delete their own analyses that are not statused as complete

  • View all fire behavior analyses in WFDSS

  • Copy and run fire behavior analysis on all incidents in WFDSS

  • Terminate runs

     

Geographic Area Editor

The Geographic Area Editor (GAE) role in WFDSS NextGen builds on the Fire Behavior Analyst role and will include some expanded privileges so that assisting the field is easier for the GAE. The privileges associated with this role are primarily to facilitate the responsibilities the GAEs fulfill. It is important to note that system access and roles will no longer be managed within WFDSS NextGen since it will be integrating FAMAuth and iNAP for authentication and role control respectively, so password, account management and roles will all be controlled from within the iNAP user interface, so this role has privileges in 2 systems, iNAP and WFDSS NextGen. In addition to the WFDSS Classic GAE role the WFDSS Classic Helpdesk role will also be rolled into the WFDSS NextGen GAE role to facilitate the helpdesk assisting users with password resets and re-enabling accounts. The privileges for the GAE role are listed below with the application where those privileges are implemented:

  • Resetting user passwords - iNAP

  • Can view user security question and answers - iNAP

  • Authorize Non-Federal users for system access - iNAP

  • Assign and modify user roles equal to or less privileged than the WFDSS NextGen GAE role - iNAP

  • Re-enable user accounts nationally - iNAP

  • Disable user accounts nationally - iNAP

Support

The Support role in WFDSS NextGen will be the most privileged role in WFDSS NextGen as well as in iNAP as it builds on the Geographic Area Editor Role. The new technologies and revised system architecture of WFDSS NextGen will mean, users with the Support role in NextGen will require much fewer abilities and tools at their disposal in NextGen than the Administrators and Support roles in WFDSS Classic did and that the Administrator role can be deprecated. WFDSS NextGen will use iNAP for user support related to roles and authentication, as a backup to the GAEs who will be the primary managers of user in their Geographic Area as they are now. The privileges for the support role are listed below with the application where those privileges are implemented:

  • Modify other user's profile information - WFDSS NextGen

  • Can view service transactions - WFDSS NextGen

  • Can view message queues - WFDSS NextGen

  • Manually update the NPL and the GAPL - WFDSS NextGen

  • Can send broadcast messages - WFDSS NextGen

  • Can send system emails - WFDSS NextGen

  • Can view user activity reports & logs - WFDSS NextGen

  • Assign and modify user roles equal to or less privileged than the WFDSS NextGen GAE role - iNAP

 

 

WFDSS Classic Roles Not Available in WFDSS NextGen

Dispatcher

??Why is this role no longer needed??

WFDSS NextGen displays incident data from IRWIN only. Users no longer create incidents manually. Users search for the appropriate IRWIN incident so it is no longer necessary to create incidents in WFDSS and hence this role is no longer needed.

Author

This role has been consolidated into

 

Data Manager

This role is no longer needed in WFDSS NextGen as spatial fire planning functionality is now managed by the Spatial Fire Monitoring service.

 

Super Analyst

This role is no longer needed in WFDSS NextGen as the Fire Behavior Analyst Role does not need any parsing of capability.

 

Help Desk

This role will be equivalent to a Geographic Area Editor in NextGen and so is no longer needed as a unique role. Help Desk personnel will require a GAE role because of its permissions in iNAP for password, role, and system access controls with iNAP

 

National Editor

This role is no longer needed as the geographic constraints associated with the Geographic Area Editor role have been removed so GAEs are now equivalent to National Editors.

 

Administrator

Much of the abilities associated with this role has been combined with the Support Role..
